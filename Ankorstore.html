Marketplace Reordering (Ankorstore)

Ankorstore is a European B2B marketplace connecting independent retailers with brands. Despite a strong product catalog, many merchants abandoned their carts, impacting conversion.

Problem Statement

Retailers struggled to re-order frequent items quickly. The checkout flow was cumbersome and lacked a one-click reorder option, leading to a 46% abandonment rate.

Research Questions

  • What are the main pain points in the existing checkout flow?
  • How do retailers approach re-ordering frequent items?
  • Which UX improvements will increase order completion?

Methodology

Conducted a strategy workshop with PMs and designers. Interviewed 18 retailers across FR, ES, UK using TEDW. Analyzed Hotjar session data. Ran two prototype cycles in Figma with Maze tests (24 users). Used RICE and dot-voting in co-creation sessions.

Key Findings

  • Reordering button is essential: users expect a quick reorder option.
  • Complex filters and cart layout confuse repeat orders.
  • Mono-brand checkout flows reduce cognitive load.

Analysis & Discussion

User testing showed the reorder button cut order time from 17 to 5.5 minutes, then to 3.2 minutes after refinement. Simplified cart tabs improved clarity and reduced frustrations.

Conclusion

The new reordering feature and cart redesign boosted conversion by 10% and reduced support calls by 20%. Iterative testing and stakeholder alignment were key to success. Next steps: monitor metrics and scale new UX patterns.
