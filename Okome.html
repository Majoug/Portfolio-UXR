Okoume Redesign (Enedis)

Improving user workflows and accessibility for Enedis’s core data tool

Role: UX Researcher
Team: UX Researcher, Designer, Product Owner, Developers
Timeline: Mar 2023 – Apr 2024 (5 months)

Project Overview

Problem

Okoume is Enedis’s key reference tool for network data. It suffered from data overload, complex navigation, and a non‑compliant interface.

Goals

Simplify workflows, adapt UI to RGAA and brand, and identify user pain points.

Role

Led research: interviews, workshops, usability tests, and prototype validation.

Team

Collaborated with a designer, business stakeholders, product owners, and developers.

Timeline

March 2023 to April 2024 (5 months)

Research Methods

• Kick-off workshop with stakeholders to align goals and priorities.
• User interviews (8 sessions) using TEDW for open insights.
• Tree tests to map and simplify navigation structure.
• Co-creation workshops with RICE prioritization.
• Two rounds of prototypes tested with users.

Key Findings & Insights

• Cognitive overload from too much data on each screen.
• Complex information architecture hindered quick access.
• Accessibility gaps due to non‑compliance with RGAA standards.
• Need to prioritize critical information hierarchy.

Design Process & Solutions

• Developed low‑fi and high‑fi prototypes in two cycles.
• Validated designs with end users after each iteration.
• Collaborated closely with design and development teams for alignment.

Outcomes & Impact

• Simplified workflows improved user efficiency.
• Full RGAA compliance and refreshed visual brand.
• Positive user feedback on navigation clarity.
• Delivered actionable UX reports, prototypes, and IA maps.

Learnings & Reflections

This project highlighted the value of early stakeholder alignment and iterative testing. Balancing accessibility standards with brand needs requires close collaboration and frequent user validation.
