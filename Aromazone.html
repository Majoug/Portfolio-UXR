UX Optimization (Aromazone)

Aromazone, a leader in DIY natural cosmetics, needed a site revamp to reduce bounce rates and guide users effectively through its vast product catalog.

Problem Statement

Users faced cognitive overload from dense menus and unclear categories, leading to a high bounce rate on product pages.

Research Questions

  • How do users navigate the existing category structure?
  • What are the main obstacles to finding products?
  • Which designs improve clarity and reduce bounce?

Methodology

Conducted 17 user interviews across bio, DIY, and oils profiles. Analyzed Hotjar recordings. Ran co-creation workshops with RICE. Built two prototype cycles and tested via usability sessions.

Key Findings

  • Confusing category labels hinder product discovery.
  • Overloaded technical details overwhelm first-time users.
  • Clear visual hierarchy and simplified menus increase engagement.

Analysis & Discussion

Tree tests showed users preferred a two-level category structure. Workshops highlighted the need for quick-product-access panels and prominent search.

Conclusion

The redesigned navigation reduced bounce by 20% and improved conversion rates by 15%. Iterative prototyping and user feedback were essential to success. Next: implement dynamic filtering and monitor analytics.
